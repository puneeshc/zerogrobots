Design Requirements for Zero Gravity Robots

3-D Linear Motion

The robot needs to be capable of motion in 3 dimensions. The motion should not require the force of gravity.

3-D Rotation

The robot needs to be able to rotate and orient itself in 3 dimensions without relying on gravity.

3-D Route Calculation

The robot needs to be able to calculate routes in 3 dimensions. Routes may be calculated using distance sensors or a map.

Maintain Position / Orientation

The robot’s orientation and position can change when it expands its appendages or rotates a body part — due to conservation of linear and angular momentum. The robot needs to detect and manage this.

Autonomous and Remote Controlled Operation

The robot needs to support both remote controlled and autonomous operations.

Sensors and Communication

The robot needs several types of sensors — e.g. distance sensors, cameras, gyroscopes, accelerometers. Communication needs to use UHF transmitters.
